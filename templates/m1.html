Understanding Your Circle: Phases and Changes

A comprehensive guide to the phases of the menstrual cycle—menstrual, follicular, ovulatory, and luteal—explaining the physical and hormonal changes at each stage.

Menstrual Phase

This is the start of the cycle, marked by the shedding of the uterine lining. It lasts about 3-7 days. Hormone levels, particularly estrogen and progesterone, are low.

Activities you can do during this phase:

  • Gentle yoga or stretching exercises.
  • Journaling or reflecting on your emotions.
  • Ensuring proper hydration and nutrition.

Follicular Phase

Following menstruation, the body prepares an egg for ovulation. Estrogen levels rise, promoting the growth of the uterine lining. This phase ends with ovulation.

How to make the most of this phase:

  • Engage in high-energy activities like cardio workouts.
  • Plan and set goals for the upcoming weeks.
  • Incorporate fresh fruits and vegetables into your diet.

Ovulatory Phase

This is the midpoint of the cycle when the ovary releases a mature egg. Hormones like LH surge to trigger ovulation. This phase is key for those tracking fertility.

Suggestions for this phase:

  • Try social activities and connect with others.
  • Focus on strength training and core workouts.
  • Maintain a balanced diet to support your energy levels.

Luteal Phase

Post-ovulation, the body prepares for a potential pregnancy. Progesterone levels rise, and symptoms like bloating or mood changes might occur. This phase ends with the next menstrual cycle.

Self-care tips for this phase:

  • Practice relaxation techniques such as meditation.
  • Focus on consuming magnesium-rich foods.
  • Prioritize good sleep hygiene.
